How to use

The application helps you manage your finances. After creating an account and logging in, you can enter invoices and create transactions based on them.
Thanks to the register of expenses and revenues in the application, you can control your net income and the amount of taxes payable to the office.

Functionalities

  • adding invoices/receipts and creating transactions based on them
  • creating special transactions without a receipt (VAT, tax, salary payment)
  • insight into the calculations of the balance sheet, costs, net profit, VAT, tax and in real profit
  • ability to search for transactions and receipts and delete them
  • calculation of the balance from the selected range

Calculations

  • Balance: gross income - costs - other costs (containing paid vat, tax and profit)
  • Current balance (for all time) is the state of account (balance for date range is not account state)
  • Costs: include amount (gross) of all OUT Transfers (without special transfers for vat, tax, profit)
  • Gross income: include amount (gross without vat) off all IN Transfers
  • Net balance: net amount (without vat) of all IN Transfers minus net amount of all OUT Transfers
  • Other costs: sum of paid vat, tax and profit

  • Due vat: vat values of all IN Transfers minus vat values of all OUT Transfers
  • Paid vat: sum amount of all Transfers with type Tax Out
  • Vat balance: due vat minus paid vat

  • Tax flat 19%: due: is 19% income tax of net balance (business with flat tax case)
  • Tax flat balance: due tax minus paid tax (how much else do you have to pay)
  • Due profit (case 19% flat tax): is net balance minus due incoming tax 19%
  • Remaining profit (case 19% flat tax): due profit (flat tax) minus paid profit

  • Tax rate (lump-sum): rate defined in user's data or if None: default rate 12%
  • Tax lump-sum: due: is lump-sum tax of gross-income without vat (business with lump-sum tax case)
  • Tax lump-sum balance: due tax minus paid tax (how much else do you have to pay)
  • Due profit (case lump-sum tax): is net balance minus due lump-sum tax
  • Remaining profit (case lump-sum tax): due profit (lump-sum) minus paid profit

  • Paid profit: sum amount of all Transfers with type Salary
  • Paid tax: sum amount of all Transfers with type Tax Out (there is no difference in the method of tax settlement)
